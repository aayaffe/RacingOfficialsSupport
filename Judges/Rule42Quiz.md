# Rule 42 Propulsion Quiz: Study Guide for Race Officials

**Instructions:** This is a self-marked training quiz designed for Race Officials and Judges. It tests knowledge of **RRS 42 (Propulsion)** and the **World Sailing Interpretations of Rule 42** (referenced below as *BASIC*, *ROCK*, *PUMP*, *OOCH*, and *SCULL*). 

Read each statement and determine whether it is True or False, then check the explanation.

---

### 1. Any single action of the body that clearly propels a boat is prohibited.
**Answer:** **TRUE**
> **Reference & Explanation:** RRS 42.1 & BASIC 4. Any single action of the body that clearly propels the boat is a breach of the basic rule.

### 2. A boat can scull to avoid contact with another boat.
**Answer:** **FALSE**
> **Reference & Explanation:** BASIC 3 & RRS 42.2(d). Some sailors argue that this is in conflict with the ‘acts of seamanship’ referred to in the basic rule 42.1. However, in crowded starting areas or mark roundings, if it were permitted to scull to avoid contact, many boats would scull and it would be impossible to judge if they were trying to avoid contact or sculling for tactical advantage. Since such an action is not covered by the exception, it is strictly prohibited under RRS 42.2(d).

### 3. It is permitted to repeatedly pump the sail to release the batten(s) unless this action clearly propels the boat.
**Answer:** **TRUE**
> **Reference & Explanation:** RRS 42.3(e). This rule exception was implemented specifically to solve a problem after a gybe in light air when a batten inverts and doesn’t release at the first attempt.

### 4. A boat’s crew must prevent her boat rolling when sailing downwind.
**Answer:** **FALSE**
> **Reference & Explanation:** ROCK 3. It is prohibited for the crew to *induce* rolling, but they are not required to stop natural *background* rolling caused by the conditions.

### 5. Heeling the boat to windward to facilitate bearing away is prohibited.
**Answer:** **FALSE**
> **Reference & Explanation:** RRS 42.3(a) & ROCK 6. A boat may be rolled to facilitate steering.

### 6. It is permitted to repeatedly roll the boat in flat water provided it changes course with each roll.
**Answer:** **FALSE**
> **Reference & Explanation:** ROCK 7. There have to be waves present to repeatedly use the exception found in RRS 42.3(a).

### 7. The heeling of the boat has to be consistent with the amount the boat turns.
**Answer:** **TRUE**
> **Reference & Explanation:** ROCK 6. You can roll to help turn the boat, but a big roll requires a correspondingly big turn.

### 8. While rolling the boat to windward to bear off, the boat’s crew is always permitted to do a single sheet pump.
**Answer:** **FALSE**
> **Reference & Explanation:** There is no connection between being permitted to roll the boat and being permitted to pump. A pump is only permitted if the conditions in RRS 42.3(c) (surfing/planing) exist. However, a boat won’t be penalized for a single pump unless it clearly propels the boat or is repeated in the same area on the leg.

### 9. Moving the body fore and aft in phase with the waves on the beat to windward is permitted.
**Answer:** **FALSE**
> **Reference & Explanation:** OOCH 1. Movements that cause the mainsail to repeatedly flick constitute illegal body pumping.

### 10. Surfing means rapidly accelerating down the front of a wave.
**Answer:** **TRUE**
> **Reference & Explanation:** RRS 42.3(c). This is the definition of surfing. This is not to be confused with a ‘surge’, which is a small increase in speed after a pump.

### 11. It is prohibited to make a pump in an attempt to plane or surf in marginal conditions.
**Answer:** **FALSE**
> **Reference & Explanation:** PUMP 7 & PUMP 8. A single attempt is permitted. It is only when a crew makes *three unsuccessful attempts in a row* that the action becomes prohibited.

### 12. To initiate surfing or planing a boat’s crew can pull any sail.
**Answer:** **TRUE**
> **Reference & Explanation:** RRS 42.3(c). You can pull any sail in once per wave/gust, but *only* to initiate surfing or planing.

### 13. A boat’s crew is permitted to pump to initiate surfing when she is already planing.
**Answer:** **FALSE**
> **Reference & Explanation:** PUMP 12. If a boat is already surfing or planing, further pumping to "initiate" is not permitted.

### 14. Gentle movements of the helm across the centerline of the boat that do not propel the boat or prevent it from moving astern are permitted.
**Answer:** **TRUE**
> **Reference & Explanation:** RRS 42.2(d). The tiller repeatedly crossing the centerline is not always a prohibited action; it depends entirely on the force applied and the effect on the boat.

### 15. Repeated forceful movements of the helm when a boat is above a close-hauled course and is turning to a close-hauled course are permitted.
**Answer:** **TRUE**
> **Reference & Explanation:** RRS 42.3(d) & SCULL 1. The boat must clearly be changing direction toward a close-hauled course.

### 16. After a boat collides with a mark she can scull to get clear of it.
**Answer:** **FALSE**
> **Reference & Explanation:** RRS 42.3(h). This rule allows the crew to use *force* (e.g., pushing off with a hand) to get clear of an object, but it is not permission to use the helm to *scull* to get clear of a mark.

### 17. While tacking, if a crew delays trimming her boat flat when she has reached a close-hauled course the exception allowing exaggerated body movements no longer applies.
**Answer:** **TRUE**
> **Reference & Explanation:** RRS 42.3(b) & BASIC 6. The exception permits roll tacking, but once the boat is on a close-hauled course, the crew would break RRS 42.1 (Basic Rule) if, when the boat was flattened, it was clearly propelled. At that point, the exception no longer applies.

### 18. When flag “O” is displayed, pumping, rocking and sculling are permitted.
**Answer:** **FALSE**
> **Reference & Explanation:** RRS P5(a). Sculling is *never* permitted. Any exceptions enacted when flag “O” is displayed only permit pumping, rocking, and ooching, and must be explicitly permitted by the class rules.

### 19. Class specific exceptions to rule 42 no longer apply the moment flag “R” is displayed.
**Answer:** **FALSE**
> **Reference & Explanation:** RRS P5(c). The exceptions continue to apply to a boat until she has actually *passed the mark* where the flag “R” is being displayed.

### 20. A boat may be given redress when the Race Committee fails to display the “O” flag when the wind speed exceeds a specified class limit.
**Answer:** **FALSE**
> **Reference & Explanation:** RRS P4 (Redress Limitation). Furthermore, the display of the "O" flag by the race committee is generally discretionary ("may signal"), meaning failing to do so is rarely considered an improper omission.

*This guide is intended for educational purposes for Judges, Umpires, and Race Officers to promote consistency in the application of Rule 42 on the water.*
